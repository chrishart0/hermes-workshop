## Recommendation

Use **Variant 1: “Make Hermes Read the Boring Stuff”** as the primary public flyer.

Why:

- It has the clearest memorable hook.
- The generated art directly supports the promise: Hermes handling scrolls, papers, messages, and information flows.
- It has strong upper-left and lower-left negative space for headline, event details, QR, and registration.
- It is distinct enough to stand out on a coffee shop/community-board wall.

Use **Variant 2: “Build Your First Useful AI Agent”** if the flyer needs to be more conservative or broad-appeal. It is the clearest literal value proposition.

Use **Variant 3: “The Hermes Agent Lab”** for social posts or a technical audience. It feels more workshop/lab-like, but less immediately benefit-driven than Variant 1.

## Variant notes

### 1. Make Hermes Read the Boring Stuff

**Best use:** primary print flyer.

**Strengths:**

- Most memorable headline.
- Strong mythic Hermes composition.
- Paper/message motifs reinforce the idea of delegating tedious reading.
- Ample left-side text space.

**Watch-outs:**

- Slightly busier than Variant 2; keep overlaid copy tight.
- Use a white QR card in the lower-left and avoid crowding the figure.

### 2. Build Your First Useful AI Agent

**Best use:** fallback print flyer, general audience, LinkedIn/Eventbrite-style collateral.

**Strengths:**

- Cleanest and most broadly understandable promise.
- Excellent open text space.
- Visual is calm and easy to lay out.

**Watch-outs:**

- Less distinctive as a phrase.
- Could feel closer to generic AI workshop marketing unless the Hermes art is allowed to dominate.

### 3. The Hermes Agent Lab

**Best use:** social square, workshop deck title slide, builder-focused audience.

**Strengths:**

- Technical but inviting.
- Good classical-meets-digital imagery with network/tool motifs.
- “Lab” makes it clear this is hands-on.

**Watch-outs:**

- The right side is denser; keep text on the left.
- The headline sells format more than benefit.
